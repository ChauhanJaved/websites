PDF, or Portable Document Format, is a file format created by Adobe Systems in 1993 for the exchange of documents in a manner that is independent of application software, hardware, and operating systems. It encapsulates a complete description of a fixed-layout 2D document that includes text, fonts, images, and 2D vector graphics. With the use of Acrobat 3D and various other data formats, 3D drawings can now be embedded in PDF documents. While PDF is well-suited for the storage and exchange of electronic documents, there are situations where it may not be the best format. For example, if you need to insert PDF files into a PowerPoint presentation or a Microsoft Word document, you may need to convert them to JPG format. Converting documents to JPG format can also protect them from being altered due to a lack of fonts on the user's computer, and can make it easier to work with corporate archives by providing thumbnails of the first pages of found documents. JPEG, or Joint Photographic Experts Group, is a commonly used method of lossy compression for digital images, allowing for a selectable trade-off between storage size and image quality. It can typically achieve 10:1 compression with little perceptible loss in quality.
